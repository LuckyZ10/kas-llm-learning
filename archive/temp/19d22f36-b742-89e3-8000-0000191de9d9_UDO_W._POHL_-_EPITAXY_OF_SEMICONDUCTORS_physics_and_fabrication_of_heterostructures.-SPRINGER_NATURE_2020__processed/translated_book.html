Preface（前言） to the Second Edition

The book epitaxy（外延） of Semiconductors has been well received by students and the

scientiﬁc community. The ﬁrst edition focused concisely on the very basics and

omitted quite a number of important aspects. In the second edition, I therefore

added a few new chapters and also amended the initial text. The new parts comprise

the emerging ﬁeld of organic semiconductors with their speciﬁc peculiarities and

the important in situ analysis of epitaxial（外延的） growth（外延生长）, which is today routinely imple-

mented in commercial growth systems for online monitoring and control. A Chapter（章）

on the application of surfactants is added, illustrating an additional means to control

growth and pointing out the effect of kinetics（动力学）. One new Chapter（章） is dedicated to

special techniques, which are applied by all major epitaxy（外延） methods MOVPE（金属有机气相外延）, MBE（分子束外延）,

and LPE（液相外延）. Selective area growth is widely employed in nanostructure fabrication and

epitaxial（外延的） lateral overgrowth to create layers with areas of low defect density;

migration-enhanced epitaxy（外延） is used to improve the performance of MBE（分子束外延）, and

vapor-liquid-solid growth is a popular and versatile method for fabricating nano-

wires with high quality.

I would like to thank my colleagues for critically reading parts of the new

manuscript: Armin Dadgar (Univ. Magdeburg), Mario Dähne (TU Berlin), Norbert

Esser (ISAS Berlin), and Kolja Haberland (LayTec Berlin). I am indebted to Nigel

Mason (Oxford, UK) for stimulating the inclusion of in situ sensing, and I appre-

ciate encouraging comments I received from Ganesh Balakrishnan (Univ.

Albuquerque, NM).

Preface（前言） to the First Edition

epitaxy（外延）—the growth of a crystalline（晶体的） layer（层） on a crystalline（晶体的） substrate（衬底）—represents the

basis for the fabrication of semiconductor（半导体） heterostructures（异质结构） and devices. Textbooks

on semiconductor（半导体） physics and devices usually describe the design of a

heterostructure（异质结构） and subsequently measured data of a respective realization. The

chain between these end points requires to solve many basic problems related to

physics and technology. Such steps are generally described in specialized literature

focusing on diverse aspects. Students and researchers starting in the ﬁeld need to

study papers and books on quite speciﬁc problems in a wide ﬁeld. This textbook

attempts to bridge the gap between well-established books on semiconductor（半导体）

physics on one side and texts on completed heterostructures（异质结构） like semiconductor（半导体）

devices on the other.

The book is based on a one-semester course held at Technical University of

Berlin for undergraduate and graduate students in physics and engineering physics.

It is primarily addressed to the non-specialist with some basic knowledge in solid

state and semiconductor（半导体） physics. The ﬁeld of epitaxy（外延） is rapidly evolving and

includes many materials and growth techniques. The text, therefore, focuses on

basics and important aspects of epitaxy（外延）, emphasizing particularly the physical

principles. Problems are illustrated for important semiconductors with zincblende（闪锌矿） or

wurtzite（纤锌矿） structure.

The subject matter ﬁrst covers the properties of heterostructures（异质结构）. Structural

aspects implying elasticity and strain（应变） relaxation（弛豫） by dislocations are addressed as

well as electronic properties including band alignment（能带对齐） and electronic states in

low-dimensional structures. Then, the thermodynamics（热力学） and kinetics（动力学） of epitaxial（外延的）

layer（层） growth are considered, introducing the driving force of crystallization and

paying special attention to nucleation（成核） and surface structures. Instructive examples

are given for self-organized growth of quantum dots（量子点） and wires. Afterward, aspects

of doping（掺杂）, diffusion（扩散）, and contacts are discussed. Eventually, the most important

methods used for epitaxial（外延的） growth（外延生长） are introduced: metalorganic vapor-phase epi-

taxy, MBE（分子束外延）（molecular-beam epitaxy（外延），分子束外延）, and LPE（液相外延）（liquid-phase epitaxy（外延），液相外延）.

1.1.1

Roots of epitaxy（外延）

Abstract This introductory Chapter（章） provides a brief survey on the development of

epitaxial（外延的） growth（外延生长） techniques and points out tasks for the epitaxy（外延） of device structures.

Starting from early studies of alkali-halide overgrowth in the beginning of the 20th

century, basic concepts for lattice match between layer（层） and substrate（衬底） were developed

in the late 1920s, followed by the theory of misﬁt dislocations introduced about 1950.

Major progress in epitaxy（外延） was achieved by technical improvements of the growth

techniques, namely liquid phase epitaxy（外延） in the early, and molecular beam epitaxy（外延）

and metalorganic vapor phase epitaxy（外延） in the late 1960s. Current tasks for epitaxial（外延的）

growth are often motivated by needs for the fabrication of advanced devices, aiming

to control carriers and photons.

Most semiconductor（半导体） devices fabricated today are made out of a thin stack of layers

with a typical total thickness of only some µm. Layers in such a stack differ in material

composition and may be as thin as a single atomic layer（层）. All layers are to be grown

with high perfection and composition control on a bulk crystal used as a substrate（衬底）.

The growth technique employed for coping with this task is termed epitaxy（外延）. In the

following we brieﬂy consider the historical development and illustrate typical issues

accomplished using epitaxy（外延）.

He concluded that crystalline（晶体的） growth of alkali halides was more likely to occur if

the molecular volumes of the two inter-growing materials were nearly equal. We

note that such conditions often imply a similar size of the building blocks mentioned

above and consequently a small misﬁt of the lattice constants of the two materi-

als.

The discovery of X-ray diffraction (1912) and electron diffraction (1927) by crys-

tals had a strong impact on the knowledge about crystal structure. When Louis Royer

made his seminal comprehensive studies with a wide variety of layers and substrate（衬底）

materials in 1928, he could precisely report on the effect of substrate（衬底） crystal struc-

ture on the crystalline（晶体的） orientation of the layer（层） [5]. Royer introduced the term epi-

and concluded general rules for epitaxy（外延）. He noted that oriented growth occurs only

when it involves the parallelism of two lattice planes which have lattice networks

of identical or quasi-identical form and closely similar spacing. More precisely, he

found that the differences between the lattice-network spacing (lattice parameters)

for growth of alkali halides upon other alkali halides or mica (minerals of the form

XY2-3Z4O10(OH, F)2) should be no more than 15%. Such geometrical considera-

tions are still prominent today, though it was established later that epitaxy（外延） may also

occur for much larger misﬁts.

In the 1930s, G. I. Finch and A. G. Quarrell concluded from a study of zinc oxide

on sputtered zinc that the initial layer（层） is strained in order to attain lattice matching

parallel to the interface [6]. The layer（层） lattice-parameter vertical to the interface was

also considered to be changed to maintain approximately the bulk density. They

named this phenomenon pseudomorphism. Though later the experimental evidence

was pointed out to be by no means conclusive [1], the concept of pseudomorphic

layers proved to be of basic importance for epitaxial（外延的） structures.

The introduction of the theory of misﬁt dislocations at the interface between the

substrate（衬底） and the layer（层） by F. C. Frank and Jan H. van der Merve in 1949 [7–9]

extended the pseudomorphism approach and predicted a limit for the misﬁt of pseu-

domorphic growth. The incorporation of edge dislocations allows to accommodate

misﬁt strain（应变） and consequently makes epitaxy（外延） possible also for structures with larger

misﬁt. Conclusive experimental evidence for this was provided by John W. Matthews

and co-workers in the 1960s [10, 11]. Thin metal layers on single-crystal metals with

low misﬁt proved to grow pseudomorphic, while misﬁt dislocations were generated in

thicker layers with a density depending on layer（层） thickness. Later also conditions not

included in the mentioned concepts like, e.g., interface alloying or surface energies

were found to signiﬁcantly affect epitaxial（外延的） growth（外延生长）.

The emerging semiconductor（半导体） industry in the early 1960s had a strong impact on

the interest in epitaxy（外延）. In addition, advancements in the technique of producing high

vacuum and pure materials, and progress in experimental techniques like electron

microscopy and X-ray diffraction allowed to efﬁciently develop methods for epi-

taxial growth. LPE（液相外延）（liquid-phase epitaxy（外延），液相外延） enabled epitaxial（外延的） growth（外延生长） of multilayered device

structures of high complexity like separate-conﬁnement semiconductor（半导体） lasers. The

advancement of this technology was facilitated by its similarity to the well-studied

growth of bulk single-crystals from seeded solution. In the 70s the more sophisti-

cated methods of MBE（分子束外延）（molecular-beam epitaxy（外延），分子束外延） [12] and MOVPE（金属有机气相外延）（metalorganic vapor-phase epitaxy（外延），金属有机气相外延）

emerged. These techniques opened up epitaxy（外延） far from thermodynamic equilibrium

and hence fabrication of structures with atomically sharp interfaces, which cannot

be produced near equilibrium. Understanding and control of the epitaxial（外延的） growth（外延生长）

techniques was signiﬁcantly advanced by the application of in-situ studies of the

nucleation（成核） and growth process, and by the development of computational techniques.

In the late 80s the modern techniques attained a maturity to get in the lead of device

mass-production. Today a large variety of electronic, optoelectronic, magnetic, and

superconducting layer（层） structures are fabricated using epitaxial（外延的） techniques, including

1.2.1

Convention on Use of the Term “Atom”

Crystal growth in an epitaxial（外延的） process proceeds basically in the same way as con-

ventional growth of bulk crystals. In epitaxy（外延）, however, layer（层） and substrate（衬底） differ in

the nature and strength of the chemical bond. Moreover, both materials may have a

different crystal structure and generally have an unequal lattice parameter—at least

if the temperature is varied. We may therefore say that crystals differ energetically

and geometrically in epitaxy（外延） [13]. crystalline（晶体的） deposition of different materials on

each other is also termed heteroepitaxy.

Such a deﬁnition does obviously not apply for the epitaxial（外延的） deposition on a sub-

strate of the same material. The process is usually referred to as homoepitaxy (some-

times also autoepitaxy). Let us consider a simple example to illustrate that there may

still be a difference to conventional crystal growth. epitaxy（外延） of electronic devices

is usually performed on doped substrates and often starts by depositing a layer（层） of

the same material termed homoepitaxial buffer layer（层）. doping（掺杂） often alters the lat-

tice parameter without signiﬁcantly changing the chemical bond in bulk material.

A p-type doping（掺杂） of, e.g., silicon with boron to a level of 2 × 1019 cm−3 induces a

change of the lattice parameter by −1%. An undoped layer（层） on a doped substrate（衬底） of

the same material will hence not differ energetically but geometrically. This provides

a clear distinction of homoepitaxy from conventional crystal growth. According to

this differentiation deposition of a layer（层） of the same kind and doping（掺杂） like the sub-

strate underneath should be termed crystal growth instead of epitaxy（外延）. Following the

general usage we will, however, use the term homoepitaxy less strictly. Deposition

of a layer（层） of the same material as the substrate（衬底） is usually just one of many layers

to follow in the growth process of a device structure. Furthermore, fabrication of

bulk crystals is usually performed applying a different growth regime than in epi-

taxy, allowing for much higher growth rates. Accordingly also different experimental

setups are employed. The term homoepitaxy will be used here for deposition of the

same material as the substrate（衬底）, just to distinguish from heteroepitaxy.

Solids are composed of atoms, which may be charged due to the character of the

chemical bond. When used in a general way in this book, the word “atom” denotes

both, an atom or an ion. Uncharged atoms are hence for simplicity usually not dis-

tinguished from charged atom cores in, e.g., ionic crystals or metals unless explicitly

pointed out.

epitaxy（外延） denotes the regular assembly of atoms on a crystalline（晶体的） substrate（衬底）. By inventing

the scanning tunneling microscope (STM) a particular means has been developed to

control the assembly of single atoms for forming an ordered structure. The method

uses the ﬁnite force an STM tip always exerts on an adsorbed atom (adatom) attached

to the surface of a solid [14]. The magnitude of the force can be tuned by adjusting

the voltage and the position of the tip. Since generally less force is required to move

an atom across a surface than to pull it away the tip parameters can be set to allow for

positioning an individual adatom while it remains bound to the surface. The example

given in Fig.1.2 shows iron atoms on a (111) copper surface. The initially disordered

Fe atoms were carefully positioned at chosen locations. May such procedure also

be applied to deposit an epitaxial（外延的） layer（层） onto a substrate（衬底）? Imagine a skillful operator

placing one atom per second exactly on the correct site of a layer（层）. For typically about

1015 sites per cm2 such procedure requires 31 Mio.years for a single atomic layer（层）

being deposited on one cm2. The fabrication of epitaxial（外延的） structures hence requires

other methods.

The problem of growing a layer（层） by the assembly of single atoms is comparable to

the issue of describing the behavior of a gas by formulating the equation of motion

for each atom. This cannot be accomplished for exceedingly large ensembles like

a considerable fraction of 6 × 1023 atoms present in one mole. The approach of

kinetic theory of gases hence solely describes averages of certain quantities of the

vast number of atoms in the ensemble, concluded from the behavior of one single

atom. These averages correspond to macroscopic variables. We will basically follow

a comparable approach. In epitaxial（外延的） growth（外延生长） we seek to establish favorable conditions

for atoms of a nutrient phase to ﬁnding proper lattice sites in the solid phase. The

macroscopic control parameters are governed by both thermodynamics（热力学） and kinetics（动力学）,

and their effect depends on the materials and the applied growth method.
